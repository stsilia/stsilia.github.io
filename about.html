About Me

I am a PhD student at the Instituto de Astrofísica de Canarias, where I study substellar mass objects — like brown dwarfs and planetary-mass companions.

My work explores the boundary between stars and planets, and tries to understand how these mysterious objects form and evolve. I combine observational data with modeling techniques and enjoy working across different wavelengths.

Beyond research, I'm passionate about sharing science with the public and inspiring curiosity in young scientists. I also love sketching space scenes and walking by the ocean.
